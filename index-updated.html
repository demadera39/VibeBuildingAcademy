🧬 It's in our nature

Building is human.
It always has been.

We've been making things for 40,000 years — long before machines, long before code. Philosophers call us Homo faber: man the maker. Creating isn't something we learned to do. It's something we are.

When we make something, the brain releases dopamine — the same neurochemical triggered by achievement, connection and reward. Making things builds identity. It produces flow. It gives us a sense of agency over the world around us. And it connects us to something larger than ourselves.
